About

Amani Kahua is a globally celebrated, architect, urban planner and muralist whose work spans across disciplines. Her work lives at the intersection of built environment and human rights. She transforms cities through creativity and justice. She believes cities aren't just part of the built environment, they are lived and shared. They impact the quality of our lives. She designs for a future where space belongs to everyone.

"I design cities that center care." Kahua

Her murals have adorned streets from Nairobi to Berlin. Her work centers people, especially those often erased from the urabn narrative.

"I paint stories of resistance on their walls." Kahua

She has been a fierce advocate for housing justice. An SDG ambassador for Goal 11- SUstainable Cities, with a focus on SDG intersectionality. She has developed a language access toolit for participatory planning processes.

"No justice wihtout beauty. No beauty without justice." Kahua

One of the people who have inspired her throughout her work is nobel peace price laureate Wangari Mathaai
