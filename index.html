About

Amani Kahua is a globally celebrated architect, urban planner, and muralist whose work spans across disciplines. Her work exists at the intersection of the built environment and human rights. She transforms cities through creativity and justice. She believes cities aren't just part of the built environment — they are lived and shared. She designs for a future where space belongs to everyone.

"I design cities that center care." Kahua

Her murals have adorned streets from Nairobi to Berlin. Her work centers people, especially those often erased from the urban narrative.

"I paint stories of resistance on their walls." Kahua

A fierce advocate for housing justice, Amani is an SDG ambassador for Goal 11 — Sustainable Cities, with a focus on SDG intersectionality. She has developed a language access toolkit for participatory planning processes.

"No justice without beauty. No beauty without justice." Kahua

One of the people who has inspired her throughout her work is Nobel Peace Prize laureate Wangari Maathai.

"I will be a hummingbird"

- Wangari Maathai
